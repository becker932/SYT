Health, Safety & Welfare (HS&W) POLICY STATEMENT

1. Purpose

1.1 To define the HS&W policy of the Organisation and to demonstrate the commitment of the Management and personnel to implement this policy.

1.2 To set out the policy upon which the full HS&W Management System is based.

2. References

2.1 Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974

2.2 Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, as amended

2.3 BS OHSAS 18001:2007 and BS OHSAS 18002:2008

3. Associated Documentation and Records

3.1 This Policy Statement sets out the commitment of the Management and personnel to achieving high standards of HS&W management across all Organisation activities. Reference should be made to the full Control Manual and its associated documentation and records for the comprehensive arrangements in place to implement the commitments of this Statement.

4. HS&W Policy Statement

4.1 The Chief Executive holds overall executive control and is, therefore, responsible for the conduct of the Organisation from a Health, Safety & Welfare perspective. This will include approving the vision for HS&W Management and for driving forward continual improvement. The Organisation has appointed a member of Senior Management as Responsible for HS&W to ensure that a Management representative has accountability for HS&W Management. In addition to this, the Organisation has appointed a Health and Safety Administrator (HSA) to provide support in the practical application of HS&W duties to the Organisation and employees. The Senior Manager Responsible for HS&W and the HSA are contactable via telephone or e-mail.

4.2 The management and communications structure for HS&W Management shall include the Management Committee, Chief Executive, Senior Manager Responsible for HS&W, Heads of Department, HS&W Committee and the HSA to ensure that a solid framework exists across the Organisation and that HS&W objectives and programmes are identified at all Organisational levels.

4.3 The Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974, supported by a range of topic specific subordinate legislation, imposes statutory duties on employers and employees. To enable these statutory duties to be carried out, it is the policy of the Organisation, so far as is reasonably practicable, to ensure that responsibilities for Health, Safety and Welfare are assigned, accepted and fulfilled at all levels of the Organisation; that all practicable steps are taken to manage the Health, Safety and Welfare of all employees; and to conduct the business in such a way that the health and safety of service users, visitors, contractors and the public is not put at risk.

Such arrangements shall also achieve compliance with all other HS&W related requirements to which the Organisation subscribes and are defined within the associated documentation to this Policy.

4.4 It is the Policy of the Organisation, so far as is reasonably practicable, to prevent injuries, incidents and ill-health and to ensure that:-

a) The working environment of all employees is safe and without risks to health or safety and that adequate provisions are made with regard to the facilities and arrangements for their welfare at work.

b) Machinery, equipment and systems of work are safe and without risks to the health or safety of employees, service users, contractors and any other person who may be affected with regard to any premises or operations under our control.

c) Arrangements for use, handling, storage and transport of articles and substances for use at work are safe and without risks to health or safety.

d) Adequate information is available with respect to machinery, equipment and substances used at work detailing the conditions and precautions necessary to ensure that when properly used they shall be safe and without risk to health or safety.

e) Employees are provided with such instruction, information, training and supervision as is necessary to ensure their health and safety.

f) This Policy Statement, and all supporting Policies, Procedures and Arrangements shall be reviewed and updated as and when it is necessary or appropriate and at least annually. Communication of any such changes shall be made to all employees.

4.5 To enable the Organisation to meet its statutory duties and commitments set out above, a comprehensive HS&W Management System shall be operated, which shall include written Policies, Procedures and Arrangements for all relevant areas of HS&W Management. These shall be set out in a Control Manual, which shall be adopted by the Management Committee and the Chief Executive. All employees shall be provided with adequate information, instruction and training in the contents of the Manual and in risk management issues relevant to their work.

4.6 The HS&W Management System shall include provision for the on-going review of arrangements, to ensure that continual improvement in HS&W management and performance shall be achieved.

4.7 This Policy Statement shall be presented on the Organisation’s web site to ensure that all interested parties can view this commitment to HS&W and on all staff notice boards. Parties requiring further, more detailed information on the system shall be provided with all such reasonable information by the Organisation’s HSA.

4.8 It shall be the duty of all employees at work to ensure:-

a) Reasonable steps are taken to safeguard the health and safety of themselves and of other persons who may be affected by their acts or omissions at work.

b) Co-operation with the Organisation so far as is necessary to ensure compliance with any duty or requirement imposed on the Organisation, or any other person, under any relevant statutory duties.

c) Compliance with any safe working procedures, practices or ‘rules’ set out by the Organisation and with the information, instruction and training provided.

d) Any acts or omissions, or breaches of the HS&W arrangements, that present a risk to the health or safety of any personnel are timeously reported to management.

4.9 All occupational related accidents, incidents, ill health and near misses shall be reported to the HSA, who will then report to the Chief Executive, and arrange for the appropriate recording. All such eventualities shall be appropriately investigated, reported in accordance with RIDDOR, and results presented to Senior Management.
