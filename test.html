OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH, SAFETY & WELFARE CONTROL MANUAL
SECTION: 6.17
18001 Ref: 4.4.1, 4.4.3


IMPLEMENTATION AND OPERATION
Responsibilities – Departmental HS&W Champions (Optional)

1. Purpose

1.1 It is recognised that the successful management of HS&W requires the commitment of all persons within the Organisation. The purpose of this policy is, therefore, to define the HS&W roles, responsibilities and accountability of Organisation employees, from top management throughout the management chain.

2. Definitions

2.1 ‘Accountability’: (Ref BS OHSAS 18001:2007) [relating] to the person who is liable if something is not done, does not work, or fails to achieve its purpose”.

3. References

3.1 Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974

3.2 Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, as amended

3.3 BS OHSAS 18001:2007 and BS OHSAS 18002:2008

4. Associated Documents and Records

4.1 Job Specifications / Terms and Conditions of Employment

4.2 Contractors H&S Records / Files

4.3 Employee HS&W Handbook

 

5. Responsibilities – Departmental HS&W Champions (Optional)

5.1 Departmental HS&W Champions shall act as a ‘focal point’ for all HS&W issues within the department. This shall include employee representation, whereby all departmental employees may approach the Champions with any HS&W issues and the Champion will report all such issues to the HoDs without undue delay.

5.2 Champions shall provide administrative support to the HoDs as required to facilitate the HS&W programme within their departments. However, it should be noted that the HoDs shall retain accountability for HS&W performance within their departments.

5.3 The HS&W Champions shall sign their understanding and commitment to these ideals below, which shall be reviewed every 12 months.
